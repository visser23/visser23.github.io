What OYO is, how it keeps your data private, and what to expect when you're just getting started.

General

What is OYO?

OYO is your personal AI assistant — a desktop app that runs on your computer and handles the admin work that fills your day: chasing replies, drafting emails, keeping track of what's overdue, transcribing meetings, and flagging anything that needs your attention.

Unlike a chatbot that waits for you to ask questions, OYO is proactive. It keeps an eye on things in the background and surfaces what matters when it matters. You stay in charge — nothing gets sent without your approval unless you tell it to.

Who is OYO for?

OYO is for managers and senior professionals who are genuinely stretched — in meetings most of the day, with a growing pile of things that need doing and not enough time to do them. The people who end every day knowing something slipped, and knowing tomorrow won't be any different.

Practice managers, agency leads, operations managers, project directors, consultants. People who'd love a brilliant assistant but don't have one on their desk. You don't need to be technical — if you can have a conversation, you can use OYO.

How is OYO different from ChatGPT or Copilot?

ChatGPT and Copilot are reactive — you ask a question, they answer it. They don't know anything about your world unless you paste it in, and they can't take action on your behalf.

OYO already knows your emails, your calendar, your files, and your open threads. It watches for what needs doing and acts on it — drafting replies, booking meetings, chasing overdue responses. It's the difference between a search engine and an assistant.

What are Projects in OYO?

Projects are OYO's way of handling bigger, multi-deliverable work. Instead of a single quick task, OYO can run a longer background project in multiple sessions, show live progress, and produce several outputs together (for example a strategy document, supporting research notes, and a slide deck).

You can open any project to see the activity feed, check generated files as they appear, archive old projects, or delete them when you're done.

What can OYO actually do today?

Right now, OYO can:

  • Work proactively in the background — drafting emails, flagging overdue threads, chasing replies you haven't had time to send
  • Transcribe every meeting silently and automatically, with action items pulled out at the end — without announcing it to the room
  • Type for you in any app — emails, Slack, Word, Teams — just by talking
  • Read and act in Outlook, Gmail, Slack, and Teams on your behalf
  • Learn your files, contacts, and projects from day one — so you can ask it anything and it already knows the answer
  • Draft documents, write updates, and summarise what happened in your meetings
  • Run Projects for complex asks, with a live feed and multiple deliverables saved locally
  • Do all of this under strict guardrails, with nothing sent until you approve it

Is OYO finished and ready to use?

Not quite. OYO is in early access — the core features work well, but you should expect the occasional rough edge or bug as we continue to build. We're being upfront about that rather than pretending it's polished.

OYO is designed to err on the side of caution by default — it'll flag something for your approval rather than act on a hunch. But for anything genuinely critical or irreversible, treat it like you would any new assistant: keep an eye on it while it finds its feet.

Privacy & Data

Does OYO send my data to anyone?

No. OYO runs entirely on your computer. Your emails, files, and conversations are never uploaded to any server — not ours, not anyone else's.

The only exception is when OYO sends a message to your chosen AI service (ChatGPT or Claude) to get a response. Even then, it's only the specific conversation in that moment — not your email archive or documents. If you choose to run AI fully offline, nothing leaves your machine at all.

Where does OYO store my data?

Everything OYO knows — your settings, conversation history, documents it's read, and a log of what it's done — is stored in a folder on your own computer. You can look inside it, back it up, or delete it at any time. It's yours.

Does OYO track how I use it?

No. OYO doesn't monitor how you use it, doesn't collect usage statistics, and doesn't report anything back to us. There are no analytics, no cookies, and no session recording.

If you'd like to help us improve OYO, there's an opt-in option to share anonymous usage counts (not content). It's off by default and entirely your choice.

Is there a privacy policy?

There's no privacy policy because OYO doesn't collect any personal data to govern. Nothing is uploaded, stored, or shared on our end — so there's nothing to disclose.

If you connect ChatGPT or Claude, the messages you send to those services are covered by their own privacy terms. OYO has no involvement in that relationship.

Can I see everything OYO has done?

Yes. OYO keeps a log of every action it has taken — what it did, what it proposed, and what you approved or dismissed. You can review this directly inside the app at any time.

AI you already know

Why do I need an AI account?

OYO is the assistant — it organises your work, knows your context, and handles the action-taking. But the actual AI intelligence that understands language comes from services like ChatGPT (OpenAI) or Claude (Anthropic).

Rather than charging you for that on top, we let you connect directly to an AI service you already use or pay for. You get a direct relationship with your AI provider — OYO isn't in the middle taking a cut. If you'd rather avoid cloud AI entirely, OYO can also run AI models directly on your computer at no extra cost.

Can OYO work with no internet connection?

Yes. If you choose the fully offline option, OYO runs AI models directly on your computer — no internet required for any of the thinking it does. Voice recognition also runs locally.

Obviously, checking your email or Slack still needs an internet connection, but the AI itself works entirely offline.

How are my AI account credentials stored?

Your credentials are stored in your computer's built-in secure password manager — the same place your Mac or Windows PC already keeps your other passwords. They're never stored in a plain text file that could be found and read.

Which AI service should I use?

If you already have a ChatGPT or Claude account, use that — it's the simplest option. Both work well with OYO. If you want to keep everything completely private and offline, the fully offline option is the best choice.

When in doubt, start with Claude Sonnet or ChatGPT-4o. They're both capable, well-priced, and handle OYO's tasks comfortably.

How much does the AI cost?

It depends on how much you use it, but most people running OYO day-to-day find it comes to a few dollars a month — often less than a coffee. Lighter, faster AI options cost noticeably less; more capable models cost a little more.

The fully offline option costs nothing extra, since it runs on your own hardware.

Under the Hood

What computers does OYO work on?

OYO runs on Mac (macOS 12 Monterey or later, both Intel and Apple Silicon) and Windows (Windows 10 or later, 64-bit). Linux is not currently supported.

How does OYO actually work with Outlook, Slack, and Teams?

OYO has its own built-in browser that runs separately from your personal one. You log into your tools through OYO's browser once — just like you normally would — and OYO keeps those sessions active.

When OYO needs to check your email or send a message, it uses that logged-in browser. It never touches your personal browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, etc.) in any way.

Will OYO interfere with my normal browser?

No. OYO's browser is completely separate — different profiles, different data, no connection to your personal browsing at all. What you do in Chrome or Safari is nothing to do with OYO.

Does OYO run in the background?

Yes — like Dropbox or OneDrive, OYO starts automatically when you log in and sits in your menu bar (Mac) or system tray (Windows). Closing the chat window doesn't stop it; it keeps monitoring your accounts in the background.

To fully quit OYO, choose "Quit" from the menu bar icon.

How does OYO learn about my files and projects?

You point OYO at the folders you want it to know about — your OneDrive, Google Drive, project folders, or anywhere else. OYO reads those files and builds a local understanding of what's in them, so you can ask questions about your own documents without uploading anything.

It handles Word documents, PDFs, spreadsheets, presentations, emails, and more. Everything stays on your computer.

Can OYO generate Word, Excel, PowerPoint, or PDF files?

Yes. OYO can generate real Office/PDF files directly on your computer (for example project initiation docs, budget workbooks, and slide decks).

Generated files are saved under your local Documents/OYO/outputs/ folder in tidy categories (Projects, Tasks, Chat) with dated subfolders. You can open the file or its folder directly from results.

How do Projects differ from Tasks?

Tasks are for focused, short-lived work items. Projects are for broader asks that need multi-step research and multiple outputs over a longer run.

In practice: use a Task for one thing, use a Project when you want OYO to go away and build a package of deliverables.

Is OYO open source?

Partly. The core parts of OYO that run on your machine are open source — which means independent security researchers can verify that nothing is sneakily sending your data anywhere. The bits that make OYO smart at its job (how it handles actions, judges risk, and understands context) are proprietary.

Safety & Trust

Can OYO send emails or messages without my permission?

Not by default. Out of the box, OYO prepares drafts and queues them for your review — nothing is sent until you approve it. You can look at each one, edit it, or dismiss it entirely.

At higher settings, you can let OYO handle straightforward, low-stakes tasks automatically. But anything sensitive — external contacts, financial topics, anything flagged as higher risk — always comes to you first. There's also an emergency stop button that pauses everything instantly.

How much control do I have over what OYO does?

Complete control. OYO has five settings that determine how independently it operates:

  • Watch: OYO observes quietly. Nothing suggested, nothing done.
  • Suggest: OYO tells you what it thinks needs doing. You decide everything.
  • Draft (recommended start): OYO prepares actions for your approval. Nothing happens without your say-so.
  • Act: Routine, low-stakes tasks are handled automatically. Anything sensitive still needs your approval.
  • Autopilot: OYO manages your inbox and calendar within the rules you've set.

You can change the setting at any time, including switching back to Watch mode instantly.

What's the emergency stop?

The kill switch immediately pauses everything OYO is doing — any queued actions are frozen, and OYO won't take any further steps until you re-enable it. It's always one click away from the main interface.

What happens if something goes wrong?

OYO is built to be cautious. When it's unsure, it asks rather than guesses. Every action is logged, so you can always see exactly what happened and when.

That said, OYO is early access software — bugs happen. Don't rely on it for anything genuinely critical or irreversible just yet. Always review actions before approving, especially anything going to external contacts.

How does OYO decide what needs my attention versus what it can handle itself?

Every action OYO considers gets a risk check. It looks at factors like whether the recipient is someone you know, whether the topic involves money or legal matters, whether it's going outside your organisation, and more.

Anything straightforward is marked green and can be handled automatically at higher settings. More sensitive items are marked amber or red and always come to you for a decision — no matter how autonomous a setting you've chosen.

Getting Started

What do I need to get started?

Three things:

  • A Mac or Windows computer with at least 8 GB of RAM
  • A ChatGPT or Claude account — or the option to run fully offline
  • Your normal login details for Outlook, Gmail, Slack, or Teams

That's it. No IT department, no configuration files, no setup wizard.

Does OYO cost money?

OYO itself is free to download. The only cost is your AI service — typically a few dollars a month depending on how much you use it. If you use the fully offline option, there's no additional cost at all.

How do I report issues or give feedback?

Through the project's GitHub page. We're in early access and actively building, so feedback and bug reports are genuinely useful — we read everything.

Can I use OYO for work?

Yes — it's built for exactly that. Just treat it sensibly while it's in early access: start with the draft setting, check what it's proposing before approving, and build up to higher independence settings as you get a feel for how it behaves.

How is OYO different from tools like Zapier?

Zapier is built for people who like building workflows — triggers, conditions, connections between systems. It's powerful, but it requires a technical mindset and a lot of setup time.

OYO is for people who just want things handled. You tell it what you need, it figures out the steps, and you approve the result. No flowcharts, no configuration — if you can log into it, OYO can work with it.
